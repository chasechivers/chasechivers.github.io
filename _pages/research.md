Europa 
===
## The evolution and fate of shallow water on Europa
![PIAobservation](https://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov/jpegMod/PIA03878_modest.jpg)

The surface of Europa's outer icy shell is littered with dark circular features, roughly 10 km (6 mi) in diameter, as seen in the image above (NASA photojournal image PIA03878). They appear to either uplift, depress, or completely break up the surface, but how they form is not currently clear. One hypothesis suggests that they are formed by discrete lakes of salty water placed within Europa's ice shell. If true, how long these briny environments survive and how they evolve along the way is important for understanding Europa's ice shell and ultimately its ability to support life.
