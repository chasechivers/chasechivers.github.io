Welcome!
======

I am a 4th year PhD candidate at Georgia Institute of Technology in the Earth and Atmospheric Sciences department working with [Dr. Britney Schmidt](https://schmidt.eas.gatech.edu). My [current research](research) revolves around the evolution and fate of salty liquid water pockets within Europa's global ice shell as a way to better understand the ice shell's evolution and surface geology as well as investigate the habitability of Europa and the shallow subsurface of the ice shell. I combine numerical methods with observations to construct models that can simulate the thermal, physical and geochemical properties and evolution of planetary ice shells and tie them to future observations.

I received my B.S. in Physics and a minor in Mathematics at the University of Idaho in 2017. While there, I worked with [Dr. Jason Barnes](http://barnesos.net/pro/) with spectral data from the Visual and Infrared Mapping Spectrometer (VIMS) onboard the Cassini Spacecraft. Specifically, I was surveying VIMS and Radar data of Saturn’s largest moon Titan for evidence of change in the north polar lake levels through the extent of Cassini’s mission to constrain the less-than-seasonal evaporation rates during Titan’s northern spring which would inform understanding of the contemporary and historical local and global climate. Using this same data, I created a global map of Titan to get a contextual view of features and characterize them for potential landing sites for possible future missions to Titan, such as Dragonfly. Finally, I mapped and characterized over 60 of Titan's smaller north polar lakes in the "lake district" and wrote a proposal to the International Astronomical Union where 40 of them have been named as of August 2017.

To me, outreach is an important part of engaging local communities o inspire future scientists and to better understand what science they're funding. During my time at Georgia Tech, I've been involved in planning and hosting several events with the [Center for Space Technology and Research (CSTAR)](https://cstar.gatech.edu/) including events revolving around distinguished speakers, as well as hosting an expert panel for the Mars 2020 Landing that was open to the public.
